Biography

Imam Abdul Kareem Kuyateh is a young Gambian Islamic scholar and preacher known for his strong emphasis on Tawheed (the oneness of Allah) and correct Islamic belief. He was born and raised in The Gambia, where he developed a deep interest in Islamic knowledge from a young age. He memorized the Holy Qur’an, becoming a Hafiz, and later pursued higher education. Imam Abdul Kareem obtained a Bachelor’s degree from the University of The Gambia and later earned a Master’s degree from Beijing Normal University in China. Before attending university, he also earned a Higher National Diploma in English and Social Sciences and worked as a teacher. Today, he is recognized as one of the most educated young imams in The Gambia. He runs a Daara (Islamic school) where he teaches children and young people about Islam, Qur’an recitation, and proper Islamic beliefs. Imam Abdul Kareem is widely known for preaching about pure monotheism and warning Muslims against associating partners with Allah (shirk). His sermons often encourage Muslims to follow the Qur’an and the teachings of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him). His preaching has also sparked public debates in The Gambia, particularly after a sermon criticizing certain religious practices, which led to discussions within the Muslim community about religious tolerance and interpretation of Islamic teachings. Despite controversies, he remains an influential voice among some Muslims who support his message about returning to the fundamental teachings of Islam.
